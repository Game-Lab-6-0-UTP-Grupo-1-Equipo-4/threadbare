# Publish your StoryQuest

### Why this task matters
By publishing your StoryQuest, you'll generate a persuasive overview of it, as well as you'll be giving credit where credit is due.
The Endless Access Devs and Maintainers need this information when reviewing your StoryQuest: if approved and pushed upstream, they will make the arranges to show the information you provided in the StoryQuest Elder's book.
### Steps
1. Go to Godot and open the `quest.tres` file on the FileSystem. Click on it.
![publish storyquest 1](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b40b63f1-0316-406b-b0ba-40c745df50ae)
2. After clicking on the file, you can go now to the Inspector and edit: complete the sections "Title", "Description" and "Authors":
![publish your storyquest 4](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/904f4610-f25a-429e-8bd2-ce9313eea102)
3. Save in Godot.
4. If you haven't created a new branch on Git to push this change, now it's a good time. Just in case, here's the [Git Basics: Save and Push Your Work to GitHub](https://github.com/endlessm/threadbare/discussions/816) guide.
5. As you did before, submit a PR to your teams fork ([Git Basics: Submit a Pull Request to a Forked Repository](https://github.com/endlessm/threadbare/discussions/826) guide). Now you have to wait for a peer review to get your changes merged in your fork ([Git Basics: Do a Peer Review](https://github.com/endlessm/threadbare/discussions/859) guide).

✅ After your change is approved, you're done! 
:arrow_right: Contact your instructor, mentor or an Endless Access Dev/Maintainer to tell them that your StoryQuest is ready for review!
